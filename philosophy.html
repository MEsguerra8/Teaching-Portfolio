Coming Home to Teaching

Seeing children learn and grow has always been the most meaningful part of teaching for me.

When I was a kid, I wanted to be many things.

When I was a kid, I wanted to be many things. I wanted to be a doctor, just like many young girls in my time did. I knew though that my family wasn’t rich enough to send me to medical school, so that dream fizzled away...

“I didn’t start out dreaming of being a teacher, but I fell in love with it because of the kids.”

Teaching wasn’t my dream at first, but I ended up loving being in the classroom because of the kids. Seeing that lightbulb moment became my goal in teaching...

“Seeing that lightbulb moment became my goal in teaching.”

My Personal Teaching Style

My style gravitated toward the Communicative Approach. In my class, you’ll likely find:

  • Stories, songs, and videos
  • Games and creative activities
  • Group work and presentations
  • Role‑plays that bring stories to life

My Teaching Techniques

I create a supportive and engaging classroom. In my class, I:

  • Build Relationships
  • Use Warm Language
  • Engage and Empower
  • Practice with Purpose
  • Assess and Celebrate Growth
